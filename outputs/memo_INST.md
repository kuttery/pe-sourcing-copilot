## Company Overview
Instructure is a SaaS provider of learning management and assessment platforms (Canvas and Mastery Connect) serving K-12 and higher education markets. The company operates a recurring revenue model with broad end-market exposure across the education technology sector.

## Why It May Be Attractive for PE
The company scores 4.56/5 on our deterministic attractiveness framework, reflecting solid scale ($4.8B market cap), strong cash generation (29.8% FCF margin), and meaningful leverage capacity (1.0x net debt/EBITDA). The combination of reasonable growth (11.6%), manageable valuation relative to leverage availability, and operational improvement potential suggests a viable platform investment.

## Key Strengths
- **Cash generation**: 29.8% FCF margin and 26.4% EBITDA margin indicate mature, profitable operations with substantial cash conversion.
- **Leverage capacity**: Net debt/EBITDA of 1.0x provides significant room to support accretive financing and M&A.
- **Balanced growth**: 11.6% revenue growth is steady without hypergrowth valuation multiples; the company is past early-stage volatility.
- **Operational leverage**: 260 bps gap between EBITDA and FCF margins suggests potential for working capital or capex optimization.

## Key Risks
- **Valuation entry**: 9.3x EV/Revenue is elevated for a mid-teens growth profile and may constrain IRR on entry.
- **End-market headwinds**: K-12 and higher education budgets face structural pressures; enrollment and funding trends warrant close scrutiny.
- **Competitive intensity**: Market includes both specialized competitors and broader edtech platforms; pricing and churn risk must be validated.

## Possible Value-Creation Angles
- **Margin expansion**: Target EBITDA margin expansion through sales efficiency and platform consolidation; upside exists given peer benchmarks.
- **M&A bolt-ons**: Leverage balance sheet capacity to acquire adjacent assessment, workforce development, or international edtech assets.
- **Operational efficiency**: Rationalize go-to-market costs and improve sales productivity within established customer base.

## Next Diligence Questions
1. What are current churn, net dollar retention, and win/loss dynamics? How does performance vary by segment (K-12 vs. higher ed)?
2. What is the detailed composition of the 260 bps EBITDA-to-FCF gap, and how much is discretionary?
3. What is the competitive landscape and Instructure's market share position? Are there secular or cyclical headwinds in education spending?
4. What is the debt structure, maturity profile, and covenant headroom under current leverage?
