# PAYLOCITY HOLDING CORPORATION (PCTY)
## PE SOURCING MEMO

---

### COMPANY OVERVIEW
Paylocity is a cloud-based HCM and payroll software provider serving U.S. enterprises with an integrated suite spanning payroll, HR, time & labor, talent, benefits, and finance solutions. The company generates $1.7B in annual revenue with a $5.7B market cap and demonstrated 10.5% YoY growth in a mission-critical software category.

### WHY IT MAY BE ATTRACTIVE FOR PE
Paylocity presents a compelling PE profile characterized by strong cash generation (21.3% FCF margin), modest valuation at 3.2x EV/Revenue relative to SaaS peers, and significant balance sheet capacity with net debt at -0.4x EBITDA. The combination of scale, profitability, and financial flexibility creates a platform well-suited for leveraged buyout structuring and operational improvement.

### KEY STRENGTHS
- **Recurring revenue model** with integrated product breadth that increases customer switching costs and cross-sell opportunities
- **Strong unit economics** evidenced by 21.3% FCF margins and 23.7% EBITDA margins, indicating operational leverage and pricing power
- **Fortress balance sheet** with net cash position enabling acquisition capacity and downside protection in leveraged structures
- **Mid-teen growth** at 10.5% provides stability while suggesting market share gains remain available in fragmented HCM market

### KEY RISKS
- **Competitive intensity** from larger vendors (Workday, ADP) and specialized point-solution competitors may pressure growth trajectory and pricing
- **Customer concentration** and net revenue retention rates not disclosed; critical to validate stickiness assumption underlying margin sustainability
- **Macro sensitivity** to hiring cycles and HR spending could compress utilization and reduce growth visibility during economic downturns
- **Integration execution risk** if pursuing M&A to accelerate growth or fill product gaps

### POSSIBLE VALUE-CREATION ANGLES
- **Margin expansion** through platform consolidation and operating leverage, targeting 30%+ EBITDA margins given current 23.7% base
- **International expansion** of core payroll and HR solutions leveraging existing U.S. customer relationships and global payroll offering
- **Strategic M&A** to acquire specialized talent, benefits, or finance capabilities and accelerate cross-sell within installed base
- **Pricing optimization** and upsell of premium modules (spend management, advanced analytics) to mature customer cohorts

### NEXT DILIGENCE QUESTIONS
1. What is net revenue retention (NRR) and customer concentration (top 10/top 50 customers as % of revenue)? How has churn trended?
2. What is the addressable market size for each major product module, and what is estimated penetration and win rates vs. key competitors?
3. How much of the 10.5% growth is organic vs. acquisition-driven, and what are management's growth targets over the next 3-5 years?
4. What is the cost structure breakdown (R&D, S&M, G&A as % of revenue), and where are the primary opportunities for leverage?
